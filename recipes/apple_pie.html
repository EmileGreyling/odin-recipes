Apple Pie

Description

This Apple Pie recipe is absolutely delicious! It begins with a flaky pie crust, which serves as both the base and the top layer. Inside, peeled and sliced cooking apples are combined with sugar, flour, nutmeg, and cinnamon. The mixture is then placed in the pie crust, and butter is added for richness. The top crust is sealed, brushed with beaten egg, and baked until golden brown. The result is a warm, comforting apple pie that pairs perfectly with crème fraîche, vanilla ice cream, or natural yogurt. It's a timeless treat for any occasion!

Ingredients

  • 1 (14.1 ounce) package pastry for a 9-inch double-crust pie
  • 3/4 cup white sugar, or more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 6 cups thinly sliced apples
  • 1 tablespoon butter

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C).
  2. Line a 9-inch pie dish with one pastry crust; set second one aside.
  3. Combine 3/4 cup sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl. Add more sugar if your apples are tart.
  4. Layer apple slices in the prepared pie dish, sprinkling each layer with cinnamon-sugar mixture.
  5. Dot top layer with small pieces of butter. Cover with top crust.
  6. Bake pie on the lowest rack of the preheated oven for 10 minutes. Reduce oven temperature to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) and continue baking until golden brown and filling bubbles, 30 to 35 minutes more.
  7. Serve warm or cold.
